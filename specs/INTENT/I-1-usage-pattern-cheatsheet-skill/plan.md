## Task 2 — Add a conciseness / point-form directive (`SKILL.md`)

(a) **New global authoring rule** before step 5 (or a bolded lead-in inside it):

> **Write every section in concise point form.** Short bullets, not paragraphs. Include a detail
> only if it is a future-use must-know/tip or carries the conceptual learning; cut incidental
> narration, restatement, and backstory. One idea per bullet; prefer a clause over a sentence.

(b) **De-verbose the in-template guidance** that currently models prose:
- Replace the patterns description (lines 58–60) with a point-form instruction: recurring shapes,
  one bullet each — the pattern, when you reach for it, a real-file cite (`owner/repo:path`),
  one line per bullet where possible.
- Tighten the Learnings template bullet (lines 64–69) to a one-to-two-line
  `**old model** → **new model**` form with a terse why + cite (the current 3-line example bullet
  trains verbosity).
- Keep step 6's conceptual-altitude content (lines 72–75, backs O-5) but fold the conciseness rule
  alongside it so the two invariants sit together.

## Risks / trade-offs

- **Snippet vs "no syntax dump."** Mitigated by scoping: exactly one small block under its own
  heading; the no-syntax rule re-pinned to Learnings. O-5's check is Learnings-scoped, so risk is low.
- **Hand-rewrite vs live run.** Hand-rewrite may diverge from live skill output; accepted under P-3
  — change form not facts, avoid non-deterministic live calls. The skill text is the real
  deliverable; the artifact is an exemplar.
- **Conciseness is judgment-graded.** O-4 has no mechanical threshold; depends on the grader.
  Mitigation: make the directive operational ("one idea per bullet, clause over sentence, cut
  narration") so both skill and artifact give unambiguous evidence.
- **Verification needs the live spec-check agent.** Step 5's agent-graded check is the
  authoritative signal — not pre-computable here.
